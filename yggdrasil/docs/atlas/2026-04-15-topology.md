## POST-SPRINT-069 TARGET — full-utilization, USB4-bridged

**Design goal:** every piece of hardware earns its keep. Hugin runs all big
transformers via vLLM. Munin's freed RAM becomes an extended-GPU cache for
Hugin over USB4 (~100 µs, faster than Hugin's own NVMe). Munin's 780M iGPU
hosts embeddings + reranker — workloads that shouldn't compete with Hugin's
big-LLM queue. Nothing idles.

## Why Munin's 780M + RAM are no longer idle

| Resource | Sprint 068 utilization | Sprint 069 target |
|---|---|---|
| Munin 780M iGPU | Idle | TEI (embeddings) + BGE reranker — ROCm-via-gfx1103 |
| Munin 40 GiB freed RAM | Would sit unused post-Ollama-retirement | 32 GiB Valkey = warm-remote KV tier reachable from Hugin vLLM in ~100 µs |
| Munin NVMe | 9% used (73 / 937 GiB) | Same (Hugin owns the cold NVMe tier) |
| USB4 40 Gbps fabric | Unused | KV transfer path Hugin ↔ Munin @ ~100 µs |

The key architectural insight: **Munin's DRAM is literally faster to reach
from Hugin's vLLM over USB4 than Hugin's own NVMe is.** So we insert Munin
as a warm-remote tier between local RAM and local NVMe, turning 40 GiB of
previously-wasted capacity into an effective extended-GPU cache that also
persists across Hugin restarts (because Munin's Valkey stays up).
